History and Development of the Hotmail.com Website

Hotmail was created by two employees of FirePower Systems, Sabeer Bhatia and Jack Smith. They wanted to create a free web based email service that could provide fast and easy access to email from any internet connected computer in the world. This service was conceived as an alternative to the more restrictive and costly ISP based email services that were in use at the time. Hotmail was one of the first webmail services to be available on the internet. It originally offered 2 megabytes of free storage. The website was released on July 4, 1996 under its original moniker, HoTMaiL. The name was based on the language used to write web pages, HTML. (Sharma)

Due to its innovative concept, Hotmail received funding from Draper Fisher Juvetson. Through the course of 1996 and through 1997 Hotmail proved to be very popular for those people looking for free alternatives to premium ISP based email services.(Sharma) On December 31, 1997 Microsoft announced its acquisition of Hotmail for 400 million dollars. At the time of Microsoft’s purchase, Hotmail had grown to provide services for over 8.5 million users. (Pelline) In February 1999 Hotmail had become the world’s largest webmail service with Microsoft’s announcement that it had over 30 million active users. (Microsoft)

Microsoft had difficulty transitioning Hotmail to the Microsoft operating system. Hotmail continued to operate on a combination of FreeBSD and Solaris platforms. (Foley) In 2001 Microsoft announced they had transitioned to Windows 2000. They were later forced to admit that the DNS functions still required FreeBSD. Hotmail continued to rely on UNIX servers through 2002. (Sharma) Hotmail also fell prey to several serious security flaws. These flaws began to tarnish both Hotmail and Microsoft’s image.

In 2004 Hotmail was confronted with serious competition in the form of Gmail. Gmail is a mail service provided by Google. It was faster, provided larger storage and interfaced directly with Google’s growing stable of products. This competition spurred Microsoft to introduce a redesigned version of its Hotmail service called Windows Live Hotmail. (Paul)

Microsoft continued to develop Windows Live Hotmail to include expanded browser capabilities for Google Chrome and Mozilla Firefox. In 2009, Bing was integrated into the platform. To compete with Google, Microsoft began to offer 10 GB of storage space for users and added integration with Windows Live SkyDrive and Windows Live Office in 2010. (Microsoft) By 2012 Hotmail’s time was coming to an end. As Microsoft continued to work toward more seamless integration with its other products, the decision was made to move Hotmail to Outlook.com. While Hotmail no longer exists as a platform, users who still have an @hotmail email address may continue to use it with their Outlook account. (Microsoft)

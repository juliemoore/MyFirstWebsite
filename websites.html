History and Development of Important Sites on the Web

While working at CERN in 1989, Sir Tim Berners-Lee invented the world wide web. He has made it his mission to ensure the web serves humanity and is freely available to everyone. Sir Berners-Lee is the Founding Director of the World Wide Web Foundation and Director of the World Wide Web Consortium. (History of the Web, n.d.)

The World Wide Web provides publicly accessed websites via a public Internet Protocol (IP) network, such as the Internet. A website is a collection of related pages, composed of text and typically multimedia content. Web pages are formatted with instructions, such as HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) and CSS (Cascading Style Sheet.) Web pages are often accessed through a web browser that renders the page content. (Website, n.d.)

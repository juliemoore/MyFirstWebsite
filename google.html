History and Development of the Google Website

Google’s humble beginnings started as a research project by two PhD students at Stanford University, Stanford, California in January 1996. At the time, popular search engines listed search results based on the number of times the term(s) appeared on a page. Larry Page and Sergey Brin devised a new system called PageRank. (Google, n.d.) PageRank is an algorithm used to count the number of links and the quality of the links associated with a web page to determine and rank its relevancy to the search. It is still used today as a methodology for all of Google’s web tools. (PageRank, n.d.)

The domain google.com was registered on September 15, 1997 and Page and Brin incorporated Google on September 4, 1998. Their company was based out of their friend’s garage in Menlo Park, California. (Our history in depth, n.d.) As of May 2016, Forbes rates Google as the World’s #2 most valuable brand, valued at $82.5 billion. (The world's most valuable brand, 2016)

In December 1998, PC Magazine ranked Google as the search engine of choice in the Top 100 Web Sites for 1998. (Our history in depth, n.d.) As of December 2016, Google has an 89.53% worldwide share of search engines. (Top 5 desktop, tablet and console search engines from December 2015 to December 2016, n.d.)

Google’s doodles, which began in 1998 with the Burning Man logo, have become a global fan following, sparking the Internet with theories and discussions of the meanings of the doodles. (Van Dyk, 2014)

Google’s popularity isn’t just due to its superior search results. The website’s minimalist, ad free design appeals to many consumers. The website also contains links to Google Apps and a wide range of products and services, such as Gmail, YouTube, Google Maps, Google Docs, etc.

Larry Page and Sergey Brin changed the way we search the Internet and their vision and acquisition of Internet services has changed the way we use the Internet. There is no question that Google’s dominance will continue to reign for many years to come.

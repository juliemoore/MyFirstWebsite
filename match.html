History and Development of the Match.com Website

Match.com was founded by Gary Kremen, Fran Maier, and Peng T. Ong in 1993. The site was originally built for classified advertising for newspapers under the parent company, Electric Classified, Inc. Gary Kremen and Peng T. Ong designed the system and hired business and marketing consultant Simon Glinsky to develop a business strategy, which included a free trial and subscription model. Fran Maier joined the company in 1994 as the business leader and advanced the model to promote women as an attraction for men to join. The site also included the LGTB community. The website was introduced in 1995 and promoted by Wire magazine. (Match.com, n.d.)

By October 1996, 100,000 people had registered with Match.com and 150 couples using the website had been married by 1997. (Timeline: How Match.com Got Where it Is, 2010) Showcasing marriages becomes a successful means to advertise their service.

In 2003, Match.com introduced a new version of their website that increases dating opportunities, adds more features and allows for a more interactive experience. In addition, Match.com went mobile and offered mobile users to view pictures and interact with each other. The next year, it added success.match.com to share success stories of couples married by meeting each other on the website.

For its 10th anniversary, match.com introduced Happen Magazine, the first online magazine for online dating users and partners with relationship expert Dr. Phil to create the MindFindBind program. (Timeline: How Match.com Got Where it Is, 2010) In 2010, Match.com offered a mobile app for all smartphone users and partners with Yahoo! to become their exclusive dating site. (Match.com, n.d.)

The benefits of choosing match.com are its large, private database where a user can set up a profile and view potential matches within a nearby location without having contact with the individual unless desired. The site allows the user to set up a profile with chosen preferences (religion, interests, smoking/non-smoking, etc.) and be matched with individuals with similar preferences.

Match.com continues to remain one of the top online dating sites in the world, in part from its acquisitions and partnerships with other online sites. But competition remains fierce with other online sites that are free to use, whereas Match.com offers free registration and profile development, but charges a monthly subscription cost of $16-20.

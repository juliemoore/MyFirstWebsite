UC Web Browser

The UC Browser is a mobile browser available for Android, BlackBerry, iOS, Windows Phone and several other mobile platforms. It’s primarily used on Android operating systems. UC Browser is owned by Alibaba Group of China and developed by their Internet company UCWeb. The web browser supports multi-file formatting, data compression, HTML5 web app and cloud syncing features. The web browser is available as a free download from the mobile device web app store and offers free add-ons with its download manager. UC Browser is primarily used on Android operating systems with 300 million out of its 500 million downloads. (UC Browser, n.d.)

UC's primary market is used globally in China, Russia, Africa, Southeast Asia and India. UC Browser’s Facebook page reports it has a 30% market share in India. (UC Browser Facebook Page, n.d.) StatCounter reports UC web browser as the third most globally used browser with an 8.27% market share in January 2017. (Browser Market Share Worldwide, 2017)

The UC browser faced a security controversy in May 2015 when a research organization name the Citizen Lab published a report based off a document leaked by whistleblower Edward Snowden that the Australian Signals Directorate (ASD) could hack the UC Browser and infect smartphones with Spyware due to a lack of data encryption systems. In June of 2016, the Alibaba Group responded by providing documentation to the Citizen Lab that it had fixed the data leaks and privacy breaches. (UC Browser, n.d.)

The browser’s most popular features include gesture controls to perform different actions, quickly switch tabs, voice commands, incognito mode, Facebook mode, fast downloads, Ad Block and a night mode to view web pages with little light. (UC Browser Review, n.d.) The Google Play Store gives it a 4.5-star rating and reports 13,319,994 downloads. (UC Browser - Fast Download, n.d.)

UCWeb states that the browser supports HTML5 by enhancing the U3 kernel. The browser supports the HTML5 features of audio and video rendering, geographical positioning, canvas tags, direction and gravity sensors. (Which mobile browser supports HTML) HTML5Test as of February 27, 2017, ranks UC Browser 433 out of 555 points based on measured HTML5 web standards. The highest scoring browser is the Chrome Dev with 520 out of 555 points. (How well does your browser support HTML5?, 2017) The browser uses its own rendering engine called U3 Engine. There is little information provided about the engine, but it’s reported to be based off Webkit, which is an HTML/CSS web browser rendering engine.

Impact of Virtual Reality

Virtual reality is the simulation of a three-dimensional image or environment that can be interacted with in a seemingly real or physical way. While many people think of virtual reality as a relatively new concept, it can trace its routes all the way back to the 1830s. The first virtual reality devices were stereoscopes. In the 1930s the U.S. military began using mechanical flight simulators. By the 1950’s Morton Heilig created the Sensorama. The Sensorama was an arcade style cabinet that would stimulate all the senses and provide the user with an immersive experience. However, the Sensorama didn’t allow for user interaction. (vrs.org)

The first interactive virtual reality device was created in 1969 by Myron Kruegere. This was the first computer generated environment that allowed its user to interact with it. Virtual reality as we have come to understand it today began serious development in the late 1980s and early 1990s. (vrs.org)

The challenges of virtual reality is that it makes some people feel very sick and its high cost of entry. (Pappas) While lesser version of virtual reality like Google Cardboard exist. (vr.google.com) More capable devices such as the Oculus Rift are still relatively expensive in the eyes of the average consumer. (Matthews)

Contributor: Christopher Steffen

Virtual reality has yet to hit a critical point where software has been able to catch up with available technology. While hardware development is quickly gathering momentum. The software to run on these devices still lags. Part of the problem may stem from the fact that many people still think of virtual reality devices as being for gaming only. (Matthews) In spite of these issues, virtual reality is poised to be the next big thing. Once synergy is achieved however, consumers will follow in droves. It will likely only be a short time before people will be using virtual reality in every facet of their lives.

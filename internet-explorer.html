Internet Explorer Web Browser

Microsoft has released eleven versions of its Internet Explorer (IE) web browser from 1995 to 2013. Internet Explorer 1.0 was first released August 16, 1995 as a licensed version of the Mosaic web browser. (Wilson, n.d.) Version 2.0 included HTTP cookies, Netscape SSL (Secure Socket Layer) technology, Internet newsgroups (NNTP) support, RSA (algorithm for public-key cryptography) and JavaScript support. (Vygantas, 2010)

At the time, Netscape dominated the web browser marketplace with nearly three quarters of the market share. Internet Explorer fell short in its features and user experience compared to Netscape’s browser. (Netscape, n.d.) Microsoft made great strides to compete with Netscape by introducing Internet Explorer 3.0 on August 13, 1996. IE 3.0 was now independent of the Mosaic web browser, offered free of charge and came bundled with the Windows 95 operating system. IE3 was the first web browser to begin supporting CSS, HTML Layout Control, Frames and ActiveX. Internet Explorer’s market share grew from than three percent to over twenty percent by 1997.

The release of Internet Explorer 4.0 began what would be known as the “Browser Wars.” With the release of Internet Explorer 5.0, its market share skyrocketed to forty percent and rose steadily to its heights in 2002 – 2003 of ninety five percent. Microsoft’s ability to dominate the market place was largely due to the integration of its web browser with its operating systems and the inability to uninstall Internet Explorer and code. This made it difficult for users to replace Internet Explorer with another web browser. This practice led to United Sates Department of Justice and 20 U.S. states suing Microsoft for predatory practices that excluded free marketplace competition (specifically Netscape.) Microsoft was found guilty of breaking the law in 2000 and settled the lawsuit. (History of Internet Explorer, n.d.)

Since 2006, Internet Explorer’s market share has steadily declined, in part due to its 2003 decision not to offer a stand-alone version and increased competition. Version 9.0 was released in September 2010 with an attempt to regain some of its market share by making IE comparable with features offered by Google Chrome, Firefox and Opera. The changes didn’t stop the trend and IE’s share plunged to below fifty percent in September 2010. (History of Internet Explorer, n.d.) Per StatCounter, Internet Explorer has a 4.8% share while Chrome has 52% of the market share. (Browser Marketshare Wordwide, 2017)

On September 30, 2014, Microsoft announced the new Windows 10 operating system to replace Windows 7 and 8 and along with that a new web browser name Microsoft Edge. Internet Explorer, now under at Version 11, is still included in Windows 10 and as security updates for Windows 7 and 8, but Edge is now the default browser of the operating system. (History of Internet Explorer, n.d.)

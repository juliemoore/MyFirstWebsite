Impact of New Technology

We are a global society dependent on society. New technology is advancing at a record pace. It wasn't long ago when the telephone was still attached to the wall, most people hung their clothes out to dry, video games, the PC and public Internet didn't exist and we listened to music from a record player and the radio. The explosion of computers, printers, software, file systems, the Internet, the World Wide Web, cell phones and many other technological advances have had a positive and negative impact on society.

The most notable negative impact of new technology is the effect on our environment and the cost of developing and using new technology. As demand for advanced technology increases, the impact on our environment is dangerous. We are depleting our earth of precious resources and polluting our environment with toxic chemicals. Advancements and adoption of green technology lags far behind the enormous amount of pollution. In part, it's due to the cost of recycling and the demand for new gadgets. New technology has driven our society into an abundance of consumerism and the cost is impacting people from saving for retirement.

The positive aspects of new technology are that we can be more productive, communicate in a variety of channels across the globe, greater access to global current events, a greater opportunity for a variety of entertainment, rapid mobility across the globe and the quality of life has improved with advancements in medicine.
